Submission Categories

1. Contributed Talks (10+2 minutes)

We will select 5-8 contributed talks from submissions. These should present original research related to one or more of the session's key themes:

  • Rotational evolution in open clusters
  • Stellar magnetic activity and chromospheric emission
  • Chemical abundances (Li, [C/N], [Y/Mg], etc.)
  • Asteroseismology of cluster members
  • Gyrochronology and stellar age-dating techniques
  • New observational campaigns or theoretical models

2. Research Highlights / Poster Pop-ups (2 minutes)

We will select up to 5 research highlights for rapid-fire presentations. Each presenter will have one slide to showcase their main results or highlight their poster.

These are ideal for:

  • Early-career researchers
  • Work in progress or preliminary results
  • Promoting posters presented at the main conference
  • Quick snapshots of new techniques or datasets

How to Submit

  1. Prepare your abstract (maximum 300 words)
  2. Include: Title, authors, affiliations, and contact email
  3. Specify your preferred presentation type: Contributed Talk OR Research Highlight
  4. Send to: d.chahal@qmul.ac.uk
  5. Subject line: "CS23 Open Clusters Splinter Abstract - [Your Last Name]"

Selection Process

A selection committee, composed of the organizers, will review all submissions. Selection criteria include:

  • Scientific merit and originality
  • Relevance to session themes
  • Balance of topics across the session
  • Diversity of speakers and institutions

Questions?

For any questions about abstract submission, please contact the organizing team at d.chahal@qmul.ac.uk
